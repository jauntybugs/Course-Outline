<span class="week-badge">Week 6</span>

<h1>Wireframing Fundamentals</h1>

</div>


<h2>What are Wireframes?</h2>

<p>Low-fidelity visual guides that represent the skeletal framework of a page or screen.</p>


<h2>Types of Wireframes</h2>

<div class="exercise-item">

<h3>Low-Fidelity (Paper/Sketches)</h3>

<p>Quick, rough layouts focusing on structure and content hierarchy</p>

</div>

<div class="exercise-item">

<h3>Mid-Fidelity (Digital)</h3>

<p>More detailed layouts with specific UI elements and spacing</p>

</div>

<div class="exercise-item">

<h3>High-Fidelity (Detailed)</h3>

<p>Precise layouts with actual content and measurements</p>

</div>


<div class="highlight-box">

<h3>Wireframing Best Practices</h3>

<ul>

<li>Start with pen and paper for rapid ideation</li>

<li>Focus on layout and hierarchy, not visual design</li>

<li>Use consistent conventions and patterns</li>

<li>Include annotations for functionality</li>

<li>Test early and iterate often</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>
